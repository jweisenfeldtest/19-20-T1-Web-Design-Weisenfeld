Elements of Design

There are seven main element of design. These are Color, Shape, Form, Texture, Value, Line, & Space.

Color

Color is an element made up of three properties. Hue which is the name of a color. Value deals with the lightness or darkness of a hue. Finally intensity is the quality of brightness or purity. The higher the intensity the stronger and brighter the color while the lower the intensity causes a fainter and dull look to a color.

Shape

Shape is the appearence of two-dimensional (flat) shapes in a design. Circles, rectangles, sqaures, and triangles are all considered two-dimensional shapes. This element is limited to height and width.

Form

Form is a three-dimensional, free flowing element that encloses volume. This includes height, width, and depth. General shapes include a sphere, a cube, cylinder, and pyramid.

Texture

Texture is the way something feels based on appearances or how they are perceived to feel like.

Value

Value is the lightness or darkness of tones or colors. Example white is the lightest vale; black the darkest; and in-between the two you find middle gray.

Line

Line may be two-or three-dimensional, descriptive, implied, or abstract. It creates visual movement in a piece.

Space

Space can be positive or negative areas that are ment to define or a create a sense of depth.
